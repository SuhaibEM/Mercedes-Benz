History of Mercedes-Benz

1886 - The First Car

Karl Benz invents the first petrol-powered car, the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, which is considered the first automobile.

1900s - Early Expansion

Mercedes-Benz begins to establish itself as a leading automotive brand with innovations in engineering and design.

1926 - Formation of Mercedes-Benz

Mercedes-Benz is formed through the merger of Benz & Cie. and Daimler Motoren Gesellschaft (DMG), creating a powerhouse in the automotive industry.

1930s - Golden Age

Mercedes-Benz becomes known for luxury cars and continues to innovate with advanced engineering and safety features.

1950s - 1980s - Growth and Expansion

Mercedes-Benz expands globally, introduces iconic models like the 300 SL Gullwing, and focuses on performance and luxury.

1990s - Present - Modern Era

Mercedes-Benz continues to innovate with technologies such as hybrid and electric vehicles, while maintaining its reputation for luxury, safety, and performance.

History Of Mercedes-Benz Logo

The Mercedes-Benz logo, the famous three-pointed star, is one of the most recognizable
identification symbols in the automotive world. It was first adopted in 1909 and represents
the company's ambition to dominate land, sea and air with its products. This logo is not
only a symbol of the quality and luxury for which Mercedes-Benz cars are famous,
but it is also an expression of the company's rich heritage and continuous innovation.
